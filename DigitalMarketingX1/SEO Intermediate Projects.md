## **How to Present Projects in Your Portfolio**

### **Portfolio Structure Best Practices:**

**1. Case Study Format (Recommended)**

- **Situation:** What was the challenge/opportunity?
- **Strategy:** What approach did you take and why?
- **Execution:** What specific actions did you implement?
- **Results:** What measurable outcomes did you achieve?
- **Learnings:** What would you do differently next time?

**2. Include These Elements:**

- Clear project objectives
- Your role and contributions
- Timeframe (e.g., "Completed over 6 weeks")
- Tools and methodologies used
- Data and metrics (be specific: "+47% organic traffic")
- Visual evidence (screenshots, charts, graphs)
- Before/after comparisons
- Challenges overcome

**3. Portfolio Presentation Options:**

- **Personal Website** (Best option - shows technical skills)
- **PDF Portfolio** (Easy to email, professional)
- **Notion Portfolio** (Modern, shareable, easy to update)
- **Google Slides/PowerPoint** (Good for presentations)
- **GitHub** (Technical SEO specialists - show code)

## **Common Mistakes to Avoid**

❌ **Don't Do This:**

- Present projects without measurable results
- Include only tools screenshots without analysis
- Show work without explaining your thinking
- Use only generic examples ("I did keyword research")
- Forget to document before/after states
- Skip the "why" behind your decisions

✅ **Do This Instead:**

- Lead with quantified results ("+47% organic traffic in 60 days")
- Explain your strategic thinking and methodology
- Show real data with context and insights
- Provide specific, detailed case studies
- Include visual proof (charts, screenshots, graphs)
- Tell the story: challenge → solution → results
