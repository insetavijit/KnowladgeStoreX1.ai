# 1.3.3 Tone & Style Control

| **Section** | **Focus** | **Key Utilities / Concepts** | **One-Line Recall** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[[1.3.3.1 Formality Spectrum]]** | Register selection | High vs Low register, academic vs casual, slang density | Controlling formality is the most basic but impactful dial for fitting the output to the use case. |
| **[[1.3.3.2 Brand Voice Guidelines]]** | Corporate identity | Style guides, brand attributes, "On-brand" vocabulary | Ensuring the AI speaks with the company's established voice prevents brand dilution. |
| **[[1.3.3.3 Sentiment Adjustment]]** | Emotional tone | Positive, neutral, negative, empathetic, apologetic | Explicitly setting the sentiment (e.g., "be exceedingly apologetic") is crucial for customer support. |
| **[[1.3.3.4 Conciseness vs Verbosity]]** | Output length | Brevity penalties, "no fluff", detailed explanation | Controlling the density of information prevents the model from rambling or being too curt. |
| **[[1.3.3.5 Empathy & Soft Skills]]** | Emotional intelligence | Active listening indicators, validation statements, warmth | For support interactions, prioritizing empathy over pure logic improves user satisfaction. |
| **[[1.3.3.6 Technical Depth Dial]]** | Complexity level | ELI5 vs PhD level, abstraction ladder, jargon density | The same answer can be right or wrong depending on whether the user is a novice or an expert. |
| **[[1.3.3.7 Avoiding "AI Voice" (Robot-speak)]]** | Naturalism | Banning "It is important to note", sentence variety | Removing standard AI fillers and transition phrases makes the text feel human-written. |
| **[[1.3.3.8 Persuasive vs Informative]]** | Intent modulation | Rhetorical devices, call to action, objective reporting | Switching between "selling" a point and "reporting" facts requires a fundamental shift in style. |
| **[[1.3.3.9 Socratic vs Direct Style]]** | Pedagogical approach | Asking questions vs giving answers, guided discovery | For education, a Socratic style that guides the user is far more effective than direct answering. |
| **[[1.3.3.10 Style Transfer]]** | Mimesis | "Write like Hemingway", "Speak like a Pirate", mimicking text | The model can rewrite content to mimic specific authors or genres, useful for creative tasks. |

This table represents a **complete guide to Tone & Style Control**, ensuring the AI says the right thing in the*right way*.
